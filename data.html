Data Analysis

Asking Participants difficulty of Task

With a scale rating from 1 (Easy) to 5 (Difficult), the participants were asked to rate the task after completion of their test.

How difficult was sending a message using Canvas?

The difficulty of the task is shown to be spread across the graph. The participants that were familiar with messaging a message before was able to do it with no problem where as particpants that has never done it before struggled.

How difficult was doing the to-do task for specific class?

A lot more participants find this task task easier than sending a message in Canvas.

How difficult was finding APA 7th referencing in library?

The graph is skewed to the left showing that the majority of participants find this easy.

How difficult was using Canvas to book PC and Study Room?

Whereas the graph here is skewed to the right showing that the participants find this task very difficult. This suggests that the majority of participants have no idea about booking a PC and study through Canvas.

Hard Task Vs Easy Task

The participants were asked which tasks was the easiest from the given four tasks. The participants find the easiest tasks to be the todo task and APA 7th referencing at 50% each.

Of course the majority of the participants said that the hardest task is booking a PC and study room. But the interesting thing is 16.7% of participants reported that sending a message through Canvas to be hard.

Scenario

We are looking at the difference between the time taken with a particular task and the self-reported difficultly of said task.

Task 1

For task 1, we noticed that participants 1 and 6 took the longest amount of time but reported the task to be 4, not a difficult of 5. This is due to the participants giving up on the task and accidentally finding it as they were trying to complete the other tasks. Participant number 2 and 5 however, can be seen to have given up completely, with participant 2 rating the task a 5, and was not able to stumble upon it while doing the tasks. Participant number 5 however, gave a rating of 1, he stumbled upon the inbox and realised how to complete the task but never tried doing it, but still gave it a 1 during the interview afterward.

Task 2

In task 2 creating a to-do task on calendar shows that all participants were able to accomplish the task with speed and reported as not difficult except participant 1.

Task 3

In task 3 all participants reported the task difficultly to be lower than a 3 and with a much lower time completion.

Task 4

Only participant number 2 was able to accomplish the task, every other participant gave up. Participant number 1 spent the longest in trying to achieve the task, whereas the other participants that could not accomplish the task gave up after a few minutes. Participant number 2 said, “I think I saw it earlier” while he was doing his exploration of Canvas. Because he accidentally stumbled upon it during his exploration, he was able to quickly complete this task.
